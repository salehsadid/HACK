About the Club

HACK stands for Hardware Acceleration Club of KUET. The members of HACK especially work on embedded systems development and practical hardware innovation.

Mission

To empower students with hands-on engineering experience and build impactful hardware-based solutions for real-world challenges.

Vision

To become a leading student community in Bangladesh for hardware innovation, research, and industry collaboration.

What We Do

  • Organize workshops on FPGA, microcontrollers, PCB design, and embedded programming.
  • Arrange successful workshops for KUET students from different batches.
  • Conduct hands-on project sessions using Arduino, ESP32, and different sensors.
  • Run beginner-to-advanced training sessions on hardware development tools.
  • Build collaborative projects in robotics, IoT, automation, and signal processing.
  • Host hackathons, design challenges, and innovation sprints.
  • Support members in research, publication, and competition preparation.
  • Connect students with alumni, mentors, and industry professionals.

Club Projects

Robots Developed by HACK

  • SKIM-H, the first intelligent robot of Bangladesh.
  • Multi ROBOT, where two robots can communicate with each other and make a map of an area.
  • M ROBOT, a voice-controlled robot.
  • Wayfarer, a garbage-man robot.
  • Fast Line Tracer Robot.
  • And many others.

Implemented Projects

  • Home automation.
  • Online heart-rate monitoring system.
  • Pressure and temperature monitoring system of the human body.
  • Local Area Network using headphone.
  • Portable Face Recognizer.
  • And many more.

Current Innovative and Challenging Project

  • Maze Solver Robot.

Events and Programs

  • Monthly technical seminars and guest talks.
  • Hands-on bootcamps for first-year students.
  • LFR (Line Follower Robot) workshops.
  • Robo soccer workshops and practice sessions.
  • Arduino, ESP32, and sensor-based practical workshop series.
  • Project showcase day and demo fair.
  • Inter-university hardware innovation competitions.
  • Community outreach and school-level STEM sessions.
